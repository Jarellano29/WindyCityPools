Free Public Pools in Chicago

Phase 1: Free & Public Resources

Chicago Park District – Free Indoor Pools

  • Welles Park Pool – 25-yard, 6 lanes; free open & lap swim.
  • Kosciuszko Pool – 25-yard, 6 lanes; ADA accessible.
  • Portage Park Indoor Pool – 20-yard, 5 lanes; free open swim.

Free Outdoor Pools (Seasonal)

  • Fosco Park Pool – Olympic-sized, zero-depth entry.
  • McGuane Park Pool – 25-yard, 6 lanes.
  • Ellis Park Pool – Indoor/outdoor, ADA accessible.

Lakefront Beaches

Chicago has 26 miles of free public beaches.

Major beaches: Montrose, North Avenue, 31st Street, Oak Street.

Beach Flag System

  • Green: Swimming allowed.
  • Yellow: Swim with caution.
  • Red: No swimming.

Private & Paid Options

Phase 2: Learning & Improving

Learn to Swim (Free + Low Cost)

The Chicago Park District offers a free first 10‑session block for ages 6–17 (code: FIRSTFREE).

Private Options

  • Big Blue Swim School – ~$28–$35 per weekly lesson.
  • UIC Recreation – ~$85–$160 per 5‑week session.

Events, Rentals & Lifeguarding

Phase 3: Events, Rentals & Lifeguarding

The Chicago River Swim

Date: September 20, 2026

  • Must be 18+
  • Proof of open-water swim in last 36 months
  • Application open until April 27, 2026
  • ~$100 fee if selected

Kayak & Paddleboard Rentals

Example: Urban Kayaks (Riverwalk & Monroe Harbor)

  • Renters must be 18+ with ID
  • 15–17 year olds can join guided tours
  • Or paddle tandem with an adult

Lifeguard Explorers Program

Open to Chicago teens ages 14–19.

  • Can train at 15
  • Must be 16 by July 1, 2026 to work

Skills Test

  • 200‑yard swim under 3:30
  • 20‑yard underwater swim
  • Retrieve 10 lb object from 10–14 ft
